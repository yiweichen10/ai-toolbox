What is Consensus?

Consensus is an AI-powered academic search engine designed to help researchers, students, and professionals quickly find evidence-based answers from scientific literature. Unlike traditional search tools, it extracts and synthesizes conclusions directly from peer-reviewed papers, saving users hours of manual reading. The platform is particularly valuable for evidence-based medicine, psychology, and STEM fields where credible sources are crucial.

Key Features

  • Instant Paper Insights: Get key findings from academic papers without reading full texts, with AI-generated summaries highlighting methodologies and conclusions.
  • Citation-Backed Answers: Every response includes proper academic citations, allowing users to verify sources and build bibliographies effortlessly.
  • Multi-Disciplinary Coverage: Searches across 200+ million scientific papers spanning medicine, social sciences, physics, and more with specialized filters.
  • Conversational Interface: Ask research questions in natural language (e.g., "Does intermittent fasting improve cognition?") rather than using complex academic keywords.

Pricing

Consensus offers a free plan with 20 monthly searches. Premium plans start at $10/month (billed annually) for unlimited searches, full paper access, and bookmarking. The $15/month Pro tier adds team features and API access. Compared to manual research hours saved, it offers strong value for frequent academic users, though casual researchers may find the free version sufficient.

Pros & Cons

Consensus excels at delivering trustworthy, science-backed answers faster than manual research, with particularly strong applications in healthcare and technical fields. Limitations include paywalls for full paper access and less coverage of non-English research. The interface is simpler than complex academic databases but lacks some advanced filtering options.

Consensus vs Alternatives

Compared to Google Scholar, Consensus provides synthesized answers rather than just paper lists. Versus Elicit, it offers more user-friendly explanations for non-specialists. Unlike Semantic Scholar, it focuses on conclusion extraction rather than citation networks. Consensus stands out for its balance of academic rigor and accessibility.

Best For

  • Graduate students writing literature reviews
  • Healthcare professionals seeking treatment evidence
  • Researchers validating hypotheses quickly
  • Science journalists fact-checking claims
  • Policy makers needing data-driven insights

Verdict

Consensus is a game-changer for evidence-based research, offering unique time savings without sacrificing academic rigor. While not replacing comprehensive database searches, it's invaluable for initial explorations and verification. Highly recommended for frequent research needs.
